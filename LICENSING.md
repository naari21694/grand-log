# Licensing

Grand Log is **dual-licensed**. The goal: stay genuinely open source and community-driven **forever**, while ensuring anyone who *commercializes* it gives back.

## 1. Open-source license (default — free for everyone)

Grand Log is released under the **GNU Affero General Public License v3.0 (AGPL-3.0)** — see [`LICENSE`](LICENSE).

You may use, study, modify, self-host, and build on Grand Log for free, **provided that** any modified version you distribute or offer over a network is also released under AGPL-3.0 with complete source. The community keeps the journey open.

## 2. Commercial license (if you want to capitalize on it)

If you want to build a **proprietary or closed-source** product or service on Grand Log — i.e. use it commercially *without* complying with AGPL's copyleft (you don't want to open-source your stack) — you need a **separate commercial license**.

👉 **Contact the maintainer** (V Narendra Kumar) to arrange one. *(Recommended: route commercial inquiries through a Neriq business address.)*

This is the only path to commercial/closed use, and it's how Grand Log funds staying free for everyone else.

## 3. Contributions (CLA)

To keep dual-licensing possible, contributions are accepted under a **Contributor License Agreement (CLA)**: by submitting a pull request you license your contribution to the project maintainer under terms that allow it to be offered under both the AGPL-3.0 and commercial licenses. A CLA bot will guide you on your first PR. This keeps a single, clean copyright line so the project can stay free *and* sustainable.

## 4. Trademark & names

The **"Grand Log"** name and logo are reserved by the maintainer — the code is licensed, the brand is not (don't ship confusing forks under the same name).

The crew codenames (Baratie, Log Pose, Den Den Mushi, Going Merry) are an affectionate **fan homage to _One Piece_** (© Eiichiro Oda / Shueisha / Toei Animation). They are internal codenames and personality only — **not** trademark claims, and not used as package or product names.
