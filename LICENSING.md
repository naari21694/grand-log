# Licensing

Grand Log is dual-licensed. The goal: stay free and community-driven forever, while making sure anyone who profits from it gives back.

## 1. Open-source license (default, free for everyone)

Grand Log is released under the GNU Affero General Public License v3.0 (AGPL-3.0). See [`LICENSE`](LICENSE).

You may use, study, modify, self-host, and build on Grand Log for free, provided that any modified version you distribute or offer over a network is also released under AGPL-3.0 with complete source. The community keeps the journey open.

## 2. Commercial license (if you want to profit from it)

If you want to build a proprietary or closed-source product or service on Grand Log, that is, use it commercially without complying with AGPL's copyleft (you do not want to open-source your stack), you need a separate commercial license.

Contact the maintainer to arrange one (open an issue tagged `commercial-license`).

This is the only path to commercial or closed use, and it is how Grand Log stays free for everyone else.

## 3. Contributions (CLA)

To keep dual-licensing possible, contributions are accepted under a Contributor License Agreement (CLA). By submitting a pull request you license your contribution to the project maintainer under terms that allow it to be offered under both the AGPL-3.0 and commercial licenses. A CLA bot guides you on your first PR. This keeps a single, clean copyright line so the project can stay free and sustainable.

## 4. Trademark and names

The "Grand Log" name and logo are reserved by the maintainer. The code is licensed, the brand is not, so do not ship confusing forks under the same name.

The crew codenames (Baratie, Log Pose, Den Den Mushi, Going Merry) are an affectionate fan homage to One Piece (© Eiichiro Oda, Shueisha, Toei Animation). They are internal codenames and personality only, not trademark claims, and not used as package or product names.
